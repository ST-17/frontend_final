Butakov glade

Butakovskoe gorge or simply Butakovka is a very accessible place for mountain trekking. You can come here by car directly along the gorge or come on foot from Medeo.There are two waterfalls here: the lower one, which is usually visited by everyone, and the upper one, which can be covered in snow until the end of summer.

Kok Zhailau (Kazakh: Көк Жайлау, romanized: Kók Jaılaý, sky-blue summer pasture', Russian: Кок Жайляу) is a plateau in the territory of the Ile-Alatau State National Natural Park located at an altitude of 2251.2 m.

Japanese Road

The current mountain serpentine is the remnants of a working road that was laid during the construction of HPP-1. Japanese prisoners of war, who were in the camp No. 40 stationed in Almaty in the period from 1939 to 1956, punched tunnels through the mountains.

Furmanov Peak

Furmanov Peak is the closest peak to the city, with a difficulty higher than going to Butakovka or Kok-Zhailau. Its height, according to some sources, is 2,850 meters. The history of the name of the peak is confused - someone says that it is because Furmanov lived in Verny in the 1920s, someone writes that in addition to the Komissarov Gorge.

Manchuk Mametova Lake

Lake Manshuk Mametova (another name - Lake No. 6) is located in the Zailiysky Alatau, in the Maloalmatinsky gorge, at an altitude of 3550 m above sea level, 20 km south of Almaty.The lake takes its name from the eponymous glacier named after the heroine of World War II Manshuk Mametova.
